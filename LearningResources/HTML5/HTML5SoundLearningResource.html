Fire&Forget Sound - Alex Meuer

So you want to add to sound whatever it is you’re doing with HTML5 and WebAudioAPI seemed FAR too complex for your purposes.

Consider the below html code:

<audio controls>

  <source src="my_Ogg_Sound.ogg" type="audio/ogg">

  <source src="my_MP3_Sound.mp3" type="audio/mpeg">

 <source src="my_WAV_Sound.mp3" type="audio/wav">

Your browser does not support the audio element.

</audio>

Text between <audio> tags will only be displayed if the browser does not support audio.

The <audio> tag defines an audio element and the <source> tag indicates the file path and encoding.

So lets say you want a sound to go off when something happens:

  1. You add your <audio id=”example” src=”my_sound.mp3” type=”audio/mpeg”> tag to your html.
  2. You throw a <script> tag into your html and play the file through javascript.

        To fire the sound with javascript you need to getElementById(example).

        Then just call play and you can forget all about it. SIMPLE.
